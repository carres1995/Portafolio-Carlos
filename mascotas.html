Wild Pets Gallery

Discover some of the wild animals that different cultures historically kept as companions or symbols of status and skill.

Eagle

In Mongolia, eagles are used in hunting traditions and symbolize honor, freedom, and loyalty.

Lynx

The Hopewell culture in North America historically kept lynxes as exotic pets, symbolizing status and power.

Wolf

Nomadic hunter-gatherer peoples of Eurasia and North America domesticated wolves for companionship and hunting support.

Horse

Domesticated primarily in the Eurasian steppes, horses were vital for transport, warfare, and social status.
